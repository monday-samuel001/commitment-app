You add your message right at the very top line of the screen.

The text that is already there—specifically any line starting with a #—is just a "comment" or instruction. Git will completely ignore those lines when it saves your messag
Here is how to do it:

############
//Task 2

I would still be done very soon, maybe not now, maybe not tommorow, but someday
###########

Where to type: Use the very first line of the file. You can either type over the existing "Merge branch..." text or add a new line above it.

What to type: Most people just leave the default message ("Merge branch 'main'...") as is. It's perfectly fine to keep it.

How to start typing (in Vim): Press the i key on your keyboard. This enters "Insert" mode, which lets you actually type text. You’ll see -- INSERT -- at the bottom of the screen.
